Recipe #2

  • Time: 5 hour 30 minutes
  • Serves: 15 tacos
Ingredients Quantity
dried Guajillo chiles stemmed and deseeded 6
dried Ancho chiles stemmed and deseeded 4
chuck roast cut into 4 chunks 3 Pounds
Kosher salt to taste
Ground black pepper to taste
low sodium beef broth 4 cups
vine tomatoes quartered 3
garlic peeled and smashed 8 cloves
bone-in short ribs 1 ½ pounds
dried oregano 2 teaspoons
ground coriander 1 tablespoon
ground cumin 2 teaspoons
apple cider vinegar ¼ cup
corn tortillas 15
shredded Monterey Jack cheese 2 cups
chopped yellow onion 1 cup

Step 1: Place the Guajillo and Ancho chiles in a bowl full of near-boiling water. Allow the peppers to soak for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: While the peppers soak, season the chuck roast chunks with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 3: Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large Dutch oven set over medium-high heat. In 2 batches, sear the roast on all sides, using more oil as needed. Transfer the seared chuck roast to a plate and set aside.

Step 4: Add the remaining oil, tomatoes, onion, and garlic to the pot. Cook for 2-3 minutes.

Step 5: Pour in the beef broth to deglaze the pot.

Step 6: Transfer the hydrated chiles from the bowl of water to the pot. Add the short ribs, spices, and cinnamon stick. Bring the mixture to a boil, reduce the heat, and maintain a gentle simmer for 30 minutes.

Step 7: Remove the short ribs and add to the plate with the seared roast. Skim the top of the pot to remove any impurities from the bone cooking out. Remove the cinnamon stick and discard. Transfer about 1 ½ – 2 cups

Step 8: Use an immersion blender to blend the remaining pepper and tomato mixture until smooth.

Step 9: Return the short ribs, seared beef, and any juices back to the Dutch oven. Add the orange juice, apple cider vinegar, salt, and pepper to taste. Bring to a simmer, cover, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook for at least 3-4 hours

Step 10: When the beef is cooked through, remove the short rib bones. Shred the meat with 2 forks.

Step 11: Remove the reserved braising liquid from the refrigerator. Working 1 tortilla at a time, dip the tortilla in the reserved liquid. Place the tortilla on a large skillet set over medium heat.

Step 12: Top half of the tortilla with 1-2 tablespoons of shredded cheese, 2-3 tablespoons of shredded beef, and a sprinkle of onion and cilantro. Fold the tortilla in half to close.

Step 13: Top half of the tortilla with 1-2 tablespoons of shredded cheese, 2-3 tablespoons of shredded beef, and a sprinkle of onion and cilantro. Fold the tortilla in half to close.

Step 14: Cook the tacos for 30-45 seconds on each side until crisp. Transfer to a serving platter and squeeze fresh lime juice over the top.

Step 15: Repeat with the remaining tortillas and serve with any remaining braising liquid on the side for dipping.
